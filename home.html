Batman

The Caped Crusader, the Dark Knight, the World's Greatest Detective.

Who is the Batman?

Bruce Wayne, the owner of Wayne Enterprises, developed the persona of Batman in order to fight crime in Gotham City, having sworn vengeance against the city's criminals after witnessing the murder of his parents as a child. Wayne does not possess any inhuman superpowers, instead relying on martial arts training, powerful intellect, and a large arsenal of weapons and equipment afforded to him by his vast wealth.

"I must have a disguise. Criminals are a superstitious cowardly lot. So my disguise must be able to strike terror into their hearts. I must be a creature of the night, black, terrible...a...a...a bat!"

-- Bruce Wayne, Detective Comics #33.

Who are Batman's allies?

Batman is assisted in his fight against crime in Gotham City by many allies, including:

  • Alfred Pennyworth, Bruce Wayne's butler, one of the few people who knows the true identity of Batman. He is the only other resident of Wayne's residence, Wayne Manor, and acts as a kind of father figure to Bruce Wayne.
  • Lucius Fox, Bruce Wayne's business manager and technology specialist who is responsible for developing equipment for Batman.
  • Commissioner James 'Jim' Gordon of the Gotham City Police Department, Batman's closest ally in the GCPD who is unaware of his true identity and skeptical of vigilantism, but believes that Gotham needs Batman. His daughter Barbara Gordon is also an ally of Batman, fighting crime under the vigilante persona Batgirl
  • Robin, Batman's sidekick and protégé.
  • The Justice League, a team of fellow superheroes including Superman, Wonder Woman, The Flash, and others.

Who are Batman's adversaries?

Batman's long fight against the criminal elements of Gotham have led him into conflict with a number of villanous foes, the most significant of which include:

  • The Joker, a psychopathic criminal mastermind of mysterious origins, the Joker is obsessed with Batman and represents everything that Batman is not. His colourful appearance and attire, and his theatrical manner, contrast with that of Batman, as does his tendency to murder on a whim. The Joker frequently attempts to force Batman to kill, to prove that anybody can become a monster, even a hero.
  • The Penguin, the persona of Oswald Cobblepot, a powerful figure in Gotham's criminal underworld and owner of the Iceberg Lounge nightclub. Unlike the Joker, the Penguin is completely sane, and has aspirations of acquiring wealth, power and the respect of Gotham's elite. The Penguin is also obsessed with birds and frequently sets out to steal valuable bird-related items.
  • Two-Face, the criminal persona of former district attorney Harvey Dent. Dent was previously an ally of Batman and a force for good in Gotham, however an acid attack during a court trial leaves him hideously scarred and he subsequently loses his mind. He develops an obsession with duality, making important decisions by the toss of a coin, and staging crimes based around the number 2.
  • Catwoman, the persona of Selina Kyle, a prolific burglar in Gotham. Catwoman is a skilled gymnast and combatant, and despite her villanous origins has frequently worked together with Batman, even developing a love-hate romantic relationship with him. She often appears as an antiheroine, doing dishonest things with honest motivations.
  • Bane, a powerful masked mercenary and terrorist possessing superhuman strength. Bane uses the drug venom to acquire his superhuman strength and immense physicality, and he is also a superintelligent tactician and skilled martial artist.
  • Harley Quinn, formerly the Arkham Asylum psychologist Harleen Quinzel. Quinzel was assigned to treat the Joker and eventually fell in love with the criminal mastermind, becoming an accomplice to his crimes. She adopted a similar persona to the Joker, that of Harley Quinn, and may be considered as the 'Robin' to the Joker's 'Batman'.
  • The Riddler, an intelligent criminal mastermind who is known for incorporating complex riddles, traps and puzzles into his criminal schemes. Riddler is also an accomplished computer programmer, engineer and escape artist.

    Batman Timeline

  • 1939 - Batman makes his first appearance in a comic book, in the story 'The Case of the Chemical Syndicate', from Detective Comics #27.
  • 1940 - Batman gets his own solo title comic, Batman, which also introduces the characters of Joker and Catwoman.
  • Mid-1940s - The basic elements of the Batman mythos have been established, including Batman's sidekick Robin, his origin story, and his refusal to kill enemies.
  • 1952 - Batman teams with Superman for the first time in Superman #76, the success of the story leads to a further series of stories featuring the two heroes working together.
  • 1960 - Batman debuts as a member of the Justice League in The Brave and the Bold #28.
  • 1964 - DC responds to declining sales of Batman comics by revamping the character, the "New Look" Batman debuts in Detective Comics #327.
  • 1966 - The Batman television series, starring Adam West in the title role, debuts, increasing Batman's popularity and circulation of the Batman comic, the series influences the comics to adopt a campier tone.
  • 1969 - One year after the cancellation of the television series, Batman sales begin to decline and the writers attempt to distance the comic from the campy nature of the television series.
  • 1986 - One year after Batman sales hit an all time low, the limited series The Dark Knight Returns takes the Batman character back to his dark roots, sparking a resurgence in the popularity of Batman.
  • 1989 - Tim Burton's film Batman is released featuring Michael Keaton in the title role, becoming a huge box office success, the film is later followed by three sequels, the second ('Batman Returns') also directed by Burton, while the third and fourth ('Batman Forever', starring Val Kilmer as Batman, and 'Batman & Robin', starring George Clooney as Batman.) are directed by Joel Schumacher.
  • 1993 - The 'Knightfall' story arc begins, introducing the villain Bane.
  • 2005 - Chistopher Nolan's film Batman Begins is released featuring Christian Bale in the title role, he later directs The Dark Knight and The Dark Knight Rises to complete a trilogy of critically-acclaimed and commercially successful Batman films. The same year, DC launches the All-Star Batman and Robin stand-alone series.
  • 2009 - The Batman: Arkham Asylum video game is released to critical acclaim, it is later followed by sequels Batman: Arkham City, Batman: Arkham Origins, and Batman: Arkham Knight.
  • 2011 - DC relaunches its entire line of comics in the 'new 52' reboot. The 'Night of the Owls' story arc begins the newly relaunched Batman comic.
  • 2016 - Zack Snyder's film Batman vs Superman: Dawn of Justice is released, starring Ben Affleck as Batman.
